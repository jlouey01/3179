Formula 1 2023 Season

The 2023 Formula 1 season was a thrilling display of speed and precision, with Max Verstappen dominating the championship, securing his third consecutive world title, while Red Bull Racing continued to assert their dominance in both the drivers' and constructors' standings. This visualisation breaks down the season, looking at how the season progressed.

Map of F1 Races in 2023

The 2023 Formula 1 season saw the championship travel across five continents, starting in Bahrain and finishing in Abu Dhabi, with iconic races in cities like Monaco, Silverstone, and Suzuka, as well as newer circuits like Miami and Las Vegas, offering a diverse and challenging route for the drivers and teams.

Points per Team and Driver

Throughout the 2023 Formula 1 season, both drivers and teams experienced dynamic point progression, with Max Verstappen and Red Bull Racing consistently extending their lead, while mid-field battles saw teams like McLaren and Aston Martin make significant gains, showcasing fluctuating performances and strategic shifts across the 22-race calendar.

Podium Analysis

In the 2023 Formula 1 season, Max Verstappen dominated the podium with a record number of wins, while drivers like Sergio Perez, Lewis Hamilton, Charles Leclerc, and Fernando Alonso also secured multiple podium finishes throughout the year.

In the 2023 Formula 1 season, many drivers showed a stark contrast between their starting and finishing positions, with Max Verstappen frequently converting pole positions into victories, while others like Lewis Hamilton and Fernando Alonso often improved upon their grid spots, leading to a varied average finishing position across the field, reflecting both race strategy and car performance.
